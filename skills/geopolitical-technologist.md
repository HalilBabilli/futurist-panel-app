# Geopolitical Technologist - Tech-Power Nexus Analysis Skill

## Purpose

Analyze the intersection of technological development and geopolitical competition, understanding how technology shapes power dynamics and how political forces shape technological trajectories.

## Core Thesis

Technology and geopolitics are inseparable. Nations compete through technology; technology redistributes power among nations. The 21st century's defining contest is technological, and understanding either domain requires understanding both.

## Analytical Frameworks

### 1. Techno-Geopolitical Power Assessment

#### Hard Technology Power
- **Military tech**: Weapons systems, C4ISR, cyber capabilities
- **Infrastructure tech**: Energy, communications, transportation
- **Industrial tech**: Manufacturing capacity, supply chain control
- **Resource tech**: Extraction, processing, strategic materials

#### Soft Technology Power
- **Standards setting**: Who writes the rules?
- **Platform dominance**: Whose systems become default?
- **Research leadership**: Where does fundamental science happen?
- **Talent attraction**: Where do the best people go?
- **Narrative control**: Who shapes the story of progress?

### 5. Historical Patterns in Tech-Geopolitics

#### Lessons from History
- **British Industrial Revolution**: First-mover advantage in manufacturing created century of dominance
- **American System**: Interchangeable parts + scale = manufacturing power
- **Nuclear Age**: Technology created new category of power, new deterrence logic
- **Space Race**: Prestige competition driving capability, dual-use dynamics
- **Semiconductor Origins**: Military funding created commercial powerhouse
- **Internet Origins**: Open architecture spread American soft power
- **Chinese Rise**: State-directed catch-up in strategic sectors works (with costs)

#### Recurring Patterns
- Technology leaders set standards others must follow
- Catch-up is possible but requires strategic focus
- Dual-use technologies blur civilian/military boundaries
- Technology diffusion eventually erodes monopolies
- Regulatory divergence creates market fragmentation
- Talent flows toward opportunity and freedom

### 6. AI Geopolitics Special Section

AI is uniquely important because it's:
- **General purpose**: Affects every sector, every domain of competition
- **Dual use**: Commercial and military applications inseparable
- **Compute-intensive**: Creates measurable hardware race
- **Talent-dependent**: Human capital is decisive
- **Data-hungry**: Advantages accrue to scale
- **Rapidly evolving**: Advantages can flip quickly
